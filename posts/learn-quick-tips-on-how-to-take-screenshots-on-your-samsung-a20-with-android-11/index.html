1. Overview of Taking Screenshots on Samsung A20 with Android 11#

Taking screenshots is a straightforward process with the latest version of Android on the Samsung A20. With a few quick taps, you can save a screen for later viewing or sharing. Here are the essential tips for taking a screenshot on your Samsung A20 with Android 11.

First, press the ‘Volume down’ and ‘Power’ buttons simultaneously. This will start the screenshot capture process. Keep pressing the buttons until you hear a shutter sound. The screenshot will then be captured and saved in the ‘Screenshots’ folder in the gallery.

Second, you can also take screenshots with a three-finger gesture. To enable this feature, go to the ‘Advanced features’ section in the settings, and then select ‘Motions and gestures.’ Here you can find the ‘Palm swipe to capture’ feature and enable it. This way, you can take screenshots by swiping the edge of your hand across the display.

Third, you can also take a scrolling screenshot. This feature allows you to capture a longer screenshot. To take a scrolling screenshot, just press the ‘Volume down’ and ‘Power’ buttons, and then touch the ‘Scroll capture’ message that appears at the top of the display. Keep pressing the ‘Volume down’ and ‘Power’ buttons to capture the whole page. Once the scrolling is complete, the screenshot will be saved in the ‘Screenshots’ folder.

Finally, the Samsung A20’s screenshot editor allows you to annotate, crop, and share screenshots quickly. To access the editor, open the saved screenshot from the ‘Screenshots’ folder, and a menu of editing options will appear. Use the various tools to personalize or annotate screenshots and then save or share them.

By following these tips, you’ll be able to take screenshots on your Samsung A20 easily and efficiently.
