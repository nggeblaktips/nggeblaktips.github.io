1. Check and Enable Bluetooth on both devices#

Before you begin transferring contacts from your iPhone to Android, make sure that you have checked and enabled Bluetooth on both devices. To do this, go to your iPhone Settings, search for the Bluetooth option, and switch it to On. Similarly, go to your Android device Settings, open up the Bluetooth section, and enable Bluetooth. Once done, you can start the transfer process.

Now, you must pair your devices. For this, make sure that the devices are close to one another and then open up the Bluetooth connection dialogue by clicking on the Bluetooth icon on your phone. You will then see a list of available Bluetooth devices; select your Android device to connect with it. Once your devices are connected, you will get a prompt to start the transfer process on your iPhone.

When the prompt appears, head to the option Contact Transfer, click on it, and select which contacts to transfer. To make the process easier, you can also select the option to ‘transfer all contacts’. Once selected, click on the Start Transfer tab and wait for the contacts to transfer.

At the end, a success message will appear on your screen which indicates the completion of the transfer process. After this, you will be able to access the contacts on your Android device. That’s all you need to do to transfer your contacts from your iPhone to Android easily via Bluetooth.

Once the transfer is complete, you can check the list of contacts on the Android device to make sure everything transferred successfully.

2. Transfer Contacts via Bluetooth#

If you want to transfer your contacts from an iPhone to an Android device, one of the best ways to do it is through Bluetooth. This method is convenient, easy and efficient. Here are the steps you need to take to successfully transfer your contacts from iPhone to Android.

First, you must make sure that both devices have Bluetooth turned on. On your iPhone, go to Settings, then Bluetooth and ensure that it is enabled. On your Android device, go to Settings, then Bluetooth and ensure that ‘visible’ is enabled.

Next, pair your two devices. On your iPhone, choose the Android device you want to connect to, and on your Android device, choose your iPhone. You should then be able to find the devices in the ‘Available Devices’ list on both devices.

Once the devices are paired, open a third-party app on both devices to transfer your contacts. Popular third-party apps you can use include My Contacts Backup, Contacts Transfer and Copy My Data.

Follow the instructions in the app to transfer your contacts. Once the contacts have been transferred, your contacts should now be in your Android device. Be sure to confirm that all your contacts have been transferred before you delete them from your iPhone.

3. Transfer data from iPhone to Android#

Transferring your contacts from your old iPhone to your new Android phone does not have to be difficult. In this article, we will be exploring how to easily transfer your contacts from iPhone to Android via Bluetooth.

The first step is to make sure both devices are turned on and Bluetooth is enabled. You can then go to Settings on both devices, select Bluetooth, select “pair” and accept the connection on both phones.

Once the devices are connected, you will be able to select which contacts you want to transfer. You can do this by opening your address book on the iPhone, selecting the contacts you want, and then tapping the “share” button.

The last step is to select “send via Bluetooth” from the sharing options. This will start the transfer of your contacts to your Android phone. After a few moments, the transfer will be complete.

It is worth noting that some older Android devices may not be able to transfer contacts via Bluetooth. For those devices, you will need to use an app like My Contacts Backup to transfer the contacts manually.

1. Enabling Bluetooth on iPhone#

Transferring contacts from an iPhone to an Android device can be a tricky job if done the wrong way. Thankfully, one of the simplest and quickest ways of transferring contacts from an iPhone to an Android device is by utilizing Bluetooth. Here are the steps to be taken, for a successful transfer.

First, turn on Bluetooth on both the devices by going to the respective device settings. On the iPhone, select the contacts to be transferred. Then tap on the sharing option and choose Bluetooth. It will prompt you to turn on Bluetooth and also to search for the receiving device.

On the Android device, select ‘pair new device’ and a list of available devices will be displayed. Tap on the iPhone and both the devices should be connected. Go back to the sharing option and allow the iPhone to access the contacts to be transferred.

Once the contacts have been chosen, the Android device will ask for permission to receive them. Allow it and the contacts should begin transferring within a few moments. Once the contacts have been transferred, turn off the Bluetooth on both the devices and you should be good to go.

2. Transferring Contacts to Android#

Transferring contacts between different mobile operating systems can be a tedious and difficult task. However, if you have an iPhone and an Android device, transferring contacts between them can be easily done through Bluetooth. To begin, open up the Bluetooth settings on both your iPhone and Android device and ensure that they can detect one another. Next, open the Contacts app on your iPhone and select “Share Contact”. Select the contact you want to send and tap “Share Contact”. On the Android device, you will receive a prompt asking you to accept the request, press accept and the contact information will be transferred. This process can be repeated for multiple contacts as long as the devices remain paired via Bluetooth. Once all the contacts have been sent, the connection between the two devices can be manually disconnected. This method of transferring contacts is a quick and low-cost alternative and can be done without the use of a computer.
