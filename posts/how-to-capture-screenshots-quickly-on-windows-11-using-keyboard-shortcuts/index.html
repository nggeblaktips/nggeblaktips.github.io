For starters, the simplest way to take a full screenshot of whatever is on your screen is to press the “Windows key + Print Screen” buttons at the same time. Your screen will dim briefly and a notification will appear to tell you that the screenshot has been taken and saved. This screenshot can be found in the “Pictures” folder on your computer, under the “Screenshots” sub-folder.

If you only want to take a screenshot of a specific part of the screen, you can press the “Windows key + Shift + S” keys at the same time. This will bring up a small toolbar where you can select the area of the screen you want to take a screenshot of. Once you select the area, the screenshot will be taken and saved in the same location as the full screenshot (Pictures > Screenshots).

Finally, if you want to take a quick screenshot without saving it, you can press the “Windows key + Alt + Print Screen” keys at the same time. This screenshot will be copied to your computer’s clipboard and can be pasted into any image editing software such as Microsoft Paint or Adobe Photoshop.
