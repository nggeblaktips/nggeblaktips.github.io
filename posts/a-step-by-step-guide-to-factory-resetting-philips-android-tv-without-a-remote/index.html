You can use the TV’s On-Board Diagnostic feature, also known as B-On mode, to perform a factory reset. In order to do so, you must follow some specific steps.

First, connect the power cable to the TV. Press the power button on the TV twice to enter B-On Mode. Use the TV’s volume and channel buttons to navigate the menu. Press the volume down button twice on the TV to access the settings option. Select the reset option from the list. The TV will begin the reset process which could take a few minutes.

When the reset is complete, the TV will boot up and you will be asked to confirm the reset. If you choose to confirm the reset, the TV will restart in factory default mode. To exit the reset menu and return to the home screen, press the Exit button on the TV’s remote.

A1: A factory reset is a process of restoring a device to its original settings as it was when it was first purchased. This means any personal data or settings you have applied will be cleared and you’ll be back to the device’s original state.

Q2: Why should I reset my Philips Android TV?

A2: Factory resetting your Philips Android TV can help if your TV is running slowly, has viruses or other software issues, or if you are selling the device and want to remove your personal data.

Scroll down to “Settings” and select it. Next, select “System” and then “Factory Reset”. A message will appear asking you to confirm the reset. Finally, press the “Menu” button to confirm and the reset process will start.

Q4: What will be deleted after a factory reset?

A4: Factory resetting your Philips Android TV will delete all your personal data such as apps, settings, system preferences, and any data stored in the device’s memory.

Q5: How long will it take to factory reset my Philips Android TV?

A5: On average, a factory reset process will take around 5 minutes to complete. However, this can vary depending on the size of the TV and the amount of data stored on the device.
