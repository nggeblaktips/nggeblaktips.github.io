II. How to screenshot android without volume buttons#

Taking screenshots on Android devices can be tricky if the volume buttons don’t work. Thankfully, there’s a workaround for this issue that doesn’t involve having to replace any hardware. This guide will walk you through how to take a screenshot on Android without using the volume buttons.

First, you will need to enable USB debugging on your devices. To do this, open Settings > About Phone and tap Build number seven times. Then go back to Settings and select Developer Options. Check the USB debugging box, then accept any prompts that appear.

Now, download and install the Android SDK Platforms. After the program is finished installing, run the SDK Manager, and select the SDK Platform tools. Make sure not to uncheck any of the other files during the installation.

Once the Platform tools are installed, connect your device to your computer. Open a command prompt and enter the following code, replacing “device” with the name of your device: “adb shell screencap -p /sdcard/screenshot.png”. This will save a screenshot of your screen to the “/sdcard/” directory.

Finally, to access the screenshot file, go to the internal storage of your device. You will find the file inside the “screenshot.png” folder. With the file in hand, you can now easily share your screenshots with friends, family, and colleagues.
