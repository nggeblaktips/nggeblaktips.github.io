1. Overview of Transferring Contacts#

Many of us have faced the challenge of switching from an Android phone to an iPhone. It is not easy to transfer all the contacts we have saved on our Android phone to an iPhone. This guide will show you how to manually transfer contacts from your Android device to your iPhone.

First, make sure your Android device is connected to a Wi-Fi network and then go to the ‘Settings’ page. Next, go to the ‘Accounts & Sync’ option and make sure that the ‘Contacts’ option is enabled. Once done, export the contacts to an external storage such as a USB drive or an SD card.

The second step involves syncing the contacts to your Gmail account. Import the contacts from the external storage to the Gmail account. Once it’s successfully imported, go to your iPhone and open the ‘Settings’ page. From there, choose the ‘Mail, contacts, calendars’ option and then select the ‘Add account’ option.

Next, enter your Gmail credentials and then make sure that the ‘Contacts’ option is enabled. Once it’s done, the contacts from your Gmail account will be synced to your iPhone. This is the easiest way to manually transfer contacts from an Android device to an iPhone.

2. Step-by-Step Guide to Transfer Contacts#

This guide provides step-by-step instructions on how to manually transfer contacts from your Android device to your iPhone. It involves connecting your Android phone to your computer, exporting the contacts to a CSV file, importing the contacts to your Gmail account, and then syncing those contacts to your iPhone. It is a relatively simple process that doesn’t require any third-party software. Follow the steps outlined below to successfully get all your contacts transferred from your Android phone to your iPhone.

First, plug your Android phone into your computer via a USB cable. Then, open up the Contacts app and select the contacts you need to transfer. Next, select the “Export” option and choose the “CSV” option. Save the file to your computer.

Second, log in to your Gmail account. Under the “Contacts” tab, select the “Import” option and choose the file you just exported from your Android device.

Third, open up the “Settings” app on your iPhone and then select “Contacts”. Select “Add Account” and enter your Gmail email address and password.

Finally, select “Contacts” and then select “Sync Contacts”. Your contacts from your Android device should now be synced to your iPhone. You’ve successfully transferred your contacts from your Android phone to your iPhone.
